TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test) is one of the largest fresher hiring drives in India. Clearing NQT qualifies you for TCS Ninja, Digital, and Prime tracks — three different roles with very different salary bands.

## Who can apply

- B.E, B.Tech, M.E, M.Tech, B.Sc, M.Sc, BCA, MCA students
- Final-year students or graduates from 2024, 2025, 2026 batches
- Minimum 60% aggregate across 10th, 12th, and degree
- No active backlogs at the time of applying

## Step 1 — Register on TCS iON

Go to [tcsion.com](https://www.tcsion.com) and create a candidate profile. You'll need:

- A valid email ID and phone number
- Passport-size photo (JPG, under 200 KB)
- Signature scan
- 10th, 12th, and graduation marksheets

## Step 2 — Choose your slot

TCS NQT runs in multiple slots every year. Pick the one that matches your graduation year. Don't delay — slots fill up fast, especially in metro cities.

## Step 3 — The exam pattern

NQT has four sections:

1. **Numerical Ability** — 26 questions, 40 minutes
2. **Verbal Ability** — 24 questions, 30 minutes
3. **Reasoning** — 30 questions, 50 minutes
4. **Programming Logic** — 10 questions, 20 minutes

You also get a **Coding section** (2 problems, 45 minutes) for the Digital and Prime tracks.

## Step 4 — Cutoffs to target

Rough expectations based on previous years:

- **Ninja:** ~60–65% overall
- **Digital:** ~75%+ with strong coding section
- **Prime:** ~85%+ with both coding problems solved cleanly

## Step 5 — After the test

Shortlisted candidates get an Interview Letter email. Three rounds typically:

- Technical interview
- Managerial round
- HR round

Offer letters are usually rolled out 2–4 weeks after the final round.

## Final tip

Don't wait for "the right time" to register. The earlier you complete the form, the more preferred slots you get — and TCS often releases early offers to candidates who clear NQT in the first wave.
