## TCS Hiring Process for Freshers 2026

### Company Overview

TCS National Qualifier Test (NQT) is TCS's primary hiring channel for freshers, conducting multiple drives throughout the year.

### Eligibility Criteria

#### Academic Requirements:

- 60% or above in X, XII, and B.Tech/B.E. (throughout academic career)
- No active backlogs at the time of application
- Graduation year: 2024, 2025, 2026 (depending on drive)

#### Branches Accepted:

- All engineering branches (CSE, IT, ECE, EEE, Mechanical, Civil, etc.)

#### Age Limit:

- Maximum 28 years

#### Gap Years:

- Maximum 2 years of total academic gap allowed

### Selection Process

#### Round 1: TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test)

Duration: 90 minutes
Sections:

1. Numerical Ability (26 questions - 40 minutes)

- Basic arithmetic, percentages, profit & loss
- Time and work, time and distance
- Data interpretation
- Number series

2. Reasoning Ability (30 questions - 50 minutes)

- Verbal reasoning (analogies, classification)
- Logical reasoning (puzzles, seating arrangements)
- Visual reasoning (pattern recognition)

3. Verbal Ability (24 questions)

- Reading comprehension
- Grammar and vocabulary
- Sentence completion

Cutoff: Typically 60-65% overall score (varies by demand)

Preparation Tip: TCS NQT is considered easier than other company tests.

Focus on speed and accuracy.

Practice with our Aptitude Preparation for Freshers Guide.

#### Round 2: Technical Interview

Duration: 20-30 minutes

##### Focus Areas:

##### Programming Basics:

- C, C++, or Java fundamentals
- Simple coding questions (loops, arrays, strings)
- Logic building exercises

##### Core Subjects (Branch-Specific):

- DBMS: Basic SQL queries, normalization
- Operating Systems: Processes, memory management
- Networking: OSI model, TCP/IP basics

##### Project Discussion:

- Detailed explanation of your final year project
- Technologies used and your specific contribution
- Challenges faced and solutions implemented

##### Resume-Based Questions:

- Everything mentioned in your resume is fair game
- Be prepared to explain internships, certifications, skills

##### Sample Questions:

- "Write a program to find the largest element in an array"
- "Explain the difference between primary key and foreign key"
- "What is polymorphism? Give a real-world example"
- "Tell me about your academic project"

Pro Tip: TCS technical interviews are generally moderate in difficulty.

Strong basics and honest project knowledge are sufficient.

Ensure your Resume for Freshers accurately represents your skills.

#### Round 3: Managerial/HR Interview

Duration: 15-20 minutes
What They Assess:

- Communication skills
- Cultural fit with TCS values
- Long-term commitment
- Flexibility for location and role

##### Common Questions:

- "Tell me about yourself"
- "Why TCS?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
- "Are you willing to relocate anywhere in India?"
- "Tell me about a challenging situation you handled"

## Infosys Hiring Process for Freshers 2026

### Company Overview

Infosys conducts InfyTQ (talent hunt) and regular campus drives for fresher hiring.

### Eligibility Criteria

#### Academic Requirements:

- 60% or 6.0 CGPA in X, XII, and graduation (throughout)
- No active backlogs during application
- Graduation year: 2024, 2025, 2026

#### Branches Accepted:

- All engineering branches

#### Age Limit:

- No strict age limit, but generally under 25 for freshers

### Selection Process

#### Round 1: Online Assessment Test

Duration: 3 hours total
Sections:

1. Quantitative Ability (15 questions - 25 minutes)

- Moderate to difficult level
- Focus on algebra, arithmetic, data interpretation

2. Logical Reasoning (15 questions - 25 minutes)

- Puzzles, seating arrangements
- Blood relations, direction sense
- Syllogisms

3. Verbal Ability (15 questions - 25 minutes)

- Reading comprehension (2-3 passages)
- Vocabulary and grammar

4. Coding Round (2 questions - 45 minutes)

- Problem-solving questions requiring code
- Languages allowed: C, C++, Java, Python
- Test cases must pass for full marks

Example Coding Questions:

- Array manipulation problems
- String operations
- Number-based logic building

Cutoff: Typically 65-70% in aptitude + at least 1 fully solved coding question

Key Differentiator:
Infosys coding round is more challenging than TCS.

Practice on HackerRank and LeetCode.

#### Round 2: Technical Interview

Duration: 30-45 minutes
Difficulty Level: Moderate to High

##### What to Expect:

##### In-Depth Programming:

- Data structures implementation (linked lists, trees, stacks)
- Algorithm complexity (time and space)
- Object-oriented programming concepts with code examples

##### Advanced Topics:

- DBMS: Complex queries, joins, indexing
- Operating Systems: Deadlocks, scheduling algorithms
- Networking: Protocols, security basics

##### Puzzle and Logic:

- Sometimes includes puzzle-solving rounds
- Algorithmic thinking assessment

##### Project Deep-Dive:

- Interviewers dig deep into technical architecture
- Expect questions on scalability, optimization
- Must justify technology choices

##### Sample Questions:

- "Implement a linked list reversal in your preferred language"
- "Explain how indexing improves database performance"
- "Design a system for online ticket booking"

#### Round 3: HR Interview

Duration: 20-30 minutes
Focus:

- Behavioral assessment
- Infosys values alignment
- Communication and presentation skills

##### Infosys-Specific Questions:

- "What do you know about Infosys and our recent initiatives?"
- "How do you stay updated with technology trends?"
- "Describe a time you learned something completely new"
- "What motivates you in your career?"

### Infosys Salary Package 2026

#### Standard Package:

- CTC: ₹3.6 - 4.0 LPA
- Role: Systems Engineer
- Training: 4-5 months (Mysore or Pune campus)

#### Power Programmer (Premium):

- CTC: ₹9 - 9.5 LPA
- For candidates with exceptional coding skills
- Advanced role with faster growth trajectory

#### InfyTQ (Pre-Placement):

- Students who clear InfyTQ certification get preference
- Sometimes offered higher packages

### Key Tips to Crack Infosys

- ✅ Master Coding: Strong DSA knowledge is non-negotiable
- ✅ Practice Puzzles: Prepare for logical puzzles and brain teasers
- ✅ Build Projects: Showcase hands-on coding experience through a Portfolio Website
- ✅ Stay Tech-Updated: Read about latest trends (AI, Cloud, Blockchain)
- ✅ Communication: Articulate technical concepts clearly

#### Academic Requirements:

- 60% or 6.0 CGPA throughout (X, XII, Graduation)

Duration: 120 minutes
Sections:

- Write code for standard problems (sorting, searching)

- DBMS, OS, Networks

- In-depth discussion of projects

- Approach to debugging

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q1: Can I apply to all four companies simultaneously?**

Yes, absolutely! In fact, it's recommended. Each company has different selection criteria and timelines. Applying to multiple companies increases your chances of placement. There's no restriction on accepting offers from multiple companies initially.

**Q2: Which company has the easiest selection process?**

TCS generally has the most accessible process with moderate difficulty levels and higher hiring volumes. However, "easiest" is relative—focus on preparation rather than seeking shortcuts.

**Q3: What if I don't clear the coding round but ace other sections?**

For TCS, coding is less emphasized initially. For Infosys and Wipro, coding performance is crucial. Even partial code (passing some test cases) is better than no attempt. Practice is the only solution.

**Q4: How important is CGPA in the selection process?**

CGPA determines eligibility but isn't the sole selection criterion. A 7.5 CGPA with strong skills may fare better than a 9.0 CGPA with weak practical knowledge. However, maintain 60%+ minimum for eligibility.

**Q5: Should I mention projects I did as part of coursework?**

Yes, especially if you made significant contributions beyond the basic requirements. Clearly state your role in team projects. Enhance coursework projects with additional features to make them resume-worthy.

**Q6: How do I choose which offer to accept?**

Consider:

- Immediate needs: Highest salary? Best training? Location preference?
- Long-term growth: Learning opportunities, technology exposure, career path
- Work-life balance: Company culture, work hours
- Stability vs. Innovation: TCS/Infosys for stability; Accenture for consulting exposure

**Q7: What's the typical notice period for freshers?**

Generally 60-90 days. Some companies have service bonds (Wipro: 15 months; TCS Digital: 2 years) where you must compensate if leaving early. Check your offer letter carefully.

**Q8: Can I negotiate the salary as a fresher?**

For mass recruitment, salary is generally non-negotiable as it's standardized across all freshers. However, for premium roles or if you have competing offers, polite negotiation is possible.

**Q9: How soon after joining can I switch companies?**

Technically, you can switch after completing your bond period. However, switching within 1-2 years may raise red flags for future employers. Ideally, stay 2-3 years to build foundational skills.

**Q10: What if I don't get placed in campus recruitment?**

Don't panic. Options include:

- Apply for off-campus drives (all companies conduct these)
- Target other IT companies (Cognizant, Capgemini, Tech Mahindra)
- Apply to product companies and startups
- Consider upskilling through bootcamps
- Explore government jobs or higher education

Remember: Campus placement isn't the only path to success.
